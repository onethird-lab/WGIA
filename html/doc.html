Overview

1. Goal

In the era of high-throughput technology, numerous omics data have been generated to extract meaningful biological information, such as transcriptomics, epigenomics, proteomics, and metabolomics. The analytical methods and tools are constantly improving and developing. Whole-genome interaction analysis is crucial in comprehending the regulatory mechanisms within biological pathways and networks. However, it has been limited in its prevalence due to constraints in computational resources and costs.

WGIA web server is developed as a freely accessible tool for performing large-scale differential interaction analysis based on correlation methods. Differential interaction (correlation) analyses can identify gene-gene pairs that are differentially related under case and control conditions as the following figure shows. A key feature of WGIA is the incorporation of GPU parallelization, enabling efficient computation of molecular interactions and resulting in a thousand-fold reduction in computation time compared to CPU-based methods. For example, WGIA can perform whole-genome differential interaction analysis of ~20,000 genes within 3 seconds, while it requires tens of hours on CPU.

Figure Note：Neither GENE1 nor GENE2 showed differences in the case-control study, but their relationship changed, showing a positive correlation in the case condition and a negative correlation in the control condition.

2. Workflow

Differential Interaction Analysis

Differential interaction (correlation) analyses in WGIA are based on correlation methods, including biweight midcorrelation and mutual information. Users are free to choose the method that best aligns with the characteristics of their data and their specific research objectives.

Biweight Midcorrelation

Biweight midcorrelation is a robust median-based method used for measuring linear correlation. It is less sensitive to outliers compared to pearson correlation.

Mutual Information

Mutual information relies on information theory and is utilized to captures non-linear relationships. It is applied to discrete or categorical variables. Therefore, before conducting differential interaction analysis, WGIA discretizes the continuous omics data using the equal width discretization method.

Quick Start

1. Open the WGIA start page

Access the WGIA homepage [http://www.onethird-lab.com/wgia], and click on the "START_ANALYSIS" link located in the top navigation bar.

2. Enter an email address (optional)

Fill in an email address in the user information section, but please note that this is not mandatory. If an email address is provided, the server will send notifications automatically once the analysis is done.

3. Load example data

Clicking the “Example” button will automatically upload the example datasets including omics data matrix and design matrix. Omics data matrix comprises 96 breast cancer samples and 96 normal breast samples sourced from the TCGA-BRCA project, with as well as the design matrix, where the samples and the case-control conditions are defined.

4. Launch the analysis

Click the “SUBMIT” button to start analyzing the example dataset on the WGIA web server.
